We are the African and Caribbean Society (ACS) of the University of Essex. We are a student-run organisation that aims to enlighten, entertain and support our fellow student members. We are diverse. Really. There are 45 African countries. 26 Caribbean countries.

We have members born in the UK with parents born in Africa; members born in Africa with parents born in the UK; there are students members without blood relation to the ountries but whom have an interest in the vast and rich African and Caribbean culture. This is only a small sample of the many combinations of people that we are proud to represent in ACS Essex.
The Society hold a range of regular events, showcases and panels with the aspiration of enriching the lives of everyone associated with the ACS. Enrich how? Making friends. Eating dishes from African and Caribbean countries. Participating in or attending dance-offs, panels on British Afro-Car culture. Learning the society's secret handshake. Eating African and Caribbean food. Infinities exit! Thus, we at ACS Essex will forever strive for an improved ACS Essex experience.

We welcome members from all nationalities and backgrounds to join us in our celebration, reflections and continuation of African and Caribbean culture.
